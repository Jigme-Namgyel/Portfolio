GameHive

The Product

GameHive is an e-commerce platform built for gamers to buy a wide range of gaming products, including accessories, hardware, merchandise, and collectibles. The platform aims to be a one-stop shop for gaming needs across different devices (PC, console, mobile). Users can explore, purchase, and receive their products with ease through a smooth online experience.

The Problem

There is currently no dedicated online platform for buying gaming equipment in Bhutan and nearby regions. Gamers often rely on generic e-commerce sites or scattered social media posts to find gaming products, which makes it hard to verify authenticity, compatibility, or availability. This fragmented experience results in difficulty finding suitable gear, a lack of trust in quality, and frustration over unclear pricing, delivery options, and support. Without a specialized platform, gamers lack a convenient and reliable way to purchase gaming accessories, hardware, and merchandise tailored to their needs.

The Goal

To improve user satisfaction and conversions, GameHive aims to introduce a structured and user-friendly categorization system that helps users quickly find what they’re looking for. This includes adding filters (e.g., platform, type, price), enhancing product detail pages, and simplifying the checkout process. The ultimate goal is to reduce drop-offs, increase user trust, and provide a frictionless shopping experience.

User Research: Pain Points





No Clear Categories

  • Users feel lost due to the lack of categorization (e.g., Accessories, Games, Merchandise). It’s hard to tell what’s available and relevant to their needs.

Compatibility Confusion

  • Shoppers struggle to know if a controller, headset, or part will work with their gaming system (e.g., PS5, PC).

Limited Trust and Support

  • There are no user reviews, no return policy info, and no guidance on delivery timelines, making first-time buyers nervous.

Accessbility Consideration

High Contrast & Legibility

Ensure text elements, such as plant names, prices, and care tips, have sufficient contrast against background colors and use legible, scalable fonts.

Why: Some users may struggle to read low-contrast text or decorative fonts. High contrast improves readability for everyone.

Alt Text & Labels

Ensure all images, icons, and buttons include alt text or ARIA labels for screen reader accessibility.

Why: Visually impaired users rely on screen readers to understand images and UI elements.

Conclusion

What was learned: Throughout the design and research process of the GameHive web application, valuable insights emerged about gamer behaviors and preferences. Usability studies and participant feedback emphasized the importance of clear platform-based filters (PC, PS5, Xbox), intuitive navigation, and compatibility guidance. Users appreciated the sleek interface and customization features but also highlighted areas for improvement, such as better visibility for product filters and enhanced order tracking. Staff insights further reinforced the need for streamlined backend tools to manage orders and prioritize deliveries, especially during peak hours. These findings helped refine the user experience for both shoppers and store administrators.

Next steps: Moving forward, the focus will shift to transforming the high-fidelity prototype into a fully functional, responsive web platform. This phase will involve collaboration with developers to implement all design features, ensure mobile and desktop optimization, and integrate backend order management tools. Continuous user testing will guide feature enhancements, including compatibility guides, loyalty programs, delivery scheduling tools, and real-time notifications. The goal is to deliver a seamless shopping experience that caters to all types of gamers while supporting staff with efficient management tools.
