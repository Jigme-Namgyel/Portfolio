Plant Shop App

The Product

The plant-selling platform is designed to help local plant sellers showcase their products online while providing customers with an easy way to browse, order, and receive plants. The app aims to bridge the gap between plant enthusiasts and reliable local sellers by ensuring seamless transactions and providing essential plant care information.

The Problem

Currently, plant buyers face challenges in finding healthy and affordable plants from reliable sources. Many potential customers lack knowledge about plant care, and certain plant varieties have limited local availability. On the seller’s side, showcasing products effectively and managing orders efficiently can be difficult without a structured platform.

The Goal

Develop a user-friendly mobile app that allows plant sellers to display their products attractively while enabling customers to place orders with ease. The app should support various order fulfillment options, including home delivery and self-pickup, and provide essential plant care tips to buyers.

User Research: Pain Points





Time & Availability

  • Customers struggle to find specific plants locally.
  • Limited information on availability and pricing makes purchasing inconvenient.

Order Process

  • Buyers find it difficult to place and track orders from local sellers.
  • Unclear delivery options create uncertainty about receiving orders.

Care & Maintenance

  • Customers may not have enough knowledge about plant care.
  • Sellers receive frequent inquiries about maintenance, increasing their workload.

Accessbility Consideration

High Contrast & Legibility

Ensure text elements, such as plant names, prices, and care tips, have sufficient contrast against background colors and use legible, scalable fonts.

Why: Some users may struggle to read low-contrast text or decorative fonts. High contrast improves readability for everyone.

Alt Text & Labels

Ensure all images, icons, and buttons include alt text or ARIA labels for screen reader accessibility.

Why: Visually impaired users rely on screen readers to understand images and UI elements.

Conclusion

What was learned: During the design process, many valuable insights emerged. Research and usability studies highlighted the necessity of creating an intuitive navigation system and an attractive visual design for the Plant Shop app. Continuous testing and user feedback played a vital role in enhancing the app’s features, making sure they met user expectations for exploring plants, accessing care tips, and completing purchases. Moreover, prioritizing accessibility and incorporating customization options, like personalized plant recommendations, proved essential in improving overall user satisfaction.

Next steps: Looking ahead, the priority will be to transform the high-fidelity prototype into a fully operational Plant Shop mobile app. This will require close teamwork with developers to ensure the design is effectively brought to life in a real-world setting. Ongoing user testing will be carried out to collect feedback and guide continuous improvements. Future updates might include features such as loyalty programs, personalized plant care notifications, and enhanced order tracking to further improve user experience and boost engagement.
